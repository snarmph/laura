/// THE GUIDE TO ETERNAL LIFE \\\

Some rituals are spoken of only in nightmares.
Others are buried beneath layers of silence,
sealed away by those who knew the cost of remembering.

This is one of those rituals.

They call it "transference." Others have no name for it at all.
Only scattered references remain. Burned journals, forbidden manuscripts, scribbled warnings found beside dead men with smiles carved into their cheeks.

Eternal life is not granted.
It is taken.

The first known witness was Varek the Third, writing sometime in the mid-1300s.
A scholar. A proto-scientist. A heretic.
He documented the case of a child (identity redacted, possibly erased) who underwent a sudden and unnatural transformation.

Depressed. Withdrawn.
Then, overnight, lucid. Cold. Controlled.
This change came shortly after the violent, self-inflicted death of the child’s father... in full view of the child.

Varek interviewed the boy. What the child told him was not human.

THE RITUAL:

  1. A parent births a child. Raises them.
  2. Through subtle means: abuse, manipulation, gaslighting, the parent weakens the child’s psyche. Undermines their sense of self. “Break the ego. Strip the will.”
  3. When the child’s mind is at its lowest, when identity crumbles, the parent must perform a violent suicide in front of the child. It must be immediate. Traumatic. Utterly irreversible.

This is the window.
The soul of the parent, unbound by flesh, slips into the child’s now-vacant shell.

If done perfectly, the child is erased.
The parent lives on. Reborn. Immortal.
A second life in the same blood.

BUT IF THE CHILD RESISTS...

If the child's soul clings, if even the faintest trace remains,
then the ritual fails.

Partially.

The parent enters, but does not take control.
They are trapped within the child’s mind, conscious but paralyzed.
A ghost behind the eyes. A prisoner in skin that is not theirs.

THE SECOND CHANCE:

It is not over.

  • Wait until the child is weak again. Sick. Depressed. Drugged. Grieving.
  • Then, begin the invasion.
    Twist their dreams.
    Project hallucinations.
    Remind them of the moment.
    Make them remember what they tried to forget.

When the soul fractures once more, when they begin to question what is real.
That, is your chance.

Push.
Take.
Finish what you started.

“You will not be you.
You will not be them.
You will be forever.”

— Fragment found on vellum beneath Saint Andor’s Chapel, scorched at the edges
